The umbrella

Need is all you need.

[1]

***

Jack, looking like he's lost his last quid, strolls through the landscape. It's raining cats and dogs. On his status display, his zest for life has taken a nosedive. Couldn't someone have given me a heads-up about the rain, which seems to have signed a long-term lease? I could've decked Jack out with an umbrella, sparing me the wild goose chase for materials to craft one. But that's the way the cookie crumbles in my video game, and it's oddly amusing, as long as you're not getting a soaking yourself.

***

Now, armed with this experience, I fancy myself in the shoes of an umbrella manufacturer. In my virtual realm, Jack's got more on his plate than carrying an umbrella. So, this brolly needs to be compact, easily stashable, and a doddle to handle. I reckon most of us can get on board with that.

However, considering a Berliner cracked the umbrella code over a century ago, odds are folks might already have one of these contraptions.

Does the world truly need another brolly?

***

Hold on! Backtrack a bit. Umbrella? What was the point of such a contraption again? Ah, yes, Jack doesn't fancy a soaking. It puts a damper on his spirits and dampens his joy. So, Jack needs an umbrella. In my virtual universe, I whip up that umbrella, and Jack casually tucks it into his backpack. When it rains, he pulls it out, and voila, he's sheltered from the rain.

Now, when I think of myself in similar situations, I usually pull nothing out. Why? Well, I often don't have a backpack with me. And if by some miracle I do, it's sans umbrella. I'm sure many can relate.

***

A sharing model within a city would be handy. Just like coffee cups, I could snag an umbrella from any bakery or café and return it. That would be a nifty advertising gimmick. I'd slap on some logos as paying companies fancy. The revenue would cover umbrella provision and maintenance. Bakeries and cafes offer this service as a freebie for registered customers, calling it a loyalty program. This umbrella, I'd give it a catchy name. How about Jack? Brilliant idea. But will it work?

The infrastructure is probably city-centric.

***

It might be a different story if I carried the umbrella as religiously as I put on my shoes. There was a time when folks didn't leave the house without a hat.

Although it doesn't rain every day, and trends suggest even less, the purpose here is different. I'd need to firmly anchor such a habit in our culture.

A wildly popular influencer could help with that.

They'd need to flaunt the thing constantly and look utterly smashing.

***

On second thought, maybe it's better to produce a series ("With Umbrella, Charm, and Bowler Hat - in English speaking countries well known as “The Avengers” in the early sixties"), where the main characters are always gallivanting with umbrellas. No, it's not about the "Umbrella Corporation" or "Resident Evil," and definitely not "Pan Tau" or "Arthur the Angel." No, a brand spanking new world.

The series becomes such a hit that the production costs are recouped solely through staggering viewership numbers. Extra revenue pours in through merch sales.

***

Naturally, everyone wants to carry and show off an umbrella as an accessory.

Oh, and as a fashion item, we must stock various designs.

Every garment, every occasion demands the right colors and shapes. Splendid!

More and more umbrellas grace the streets.

 

***

Our market research team missed the mark on colors and patterns. Our actual customers, those who bought an umbrella, mostly opted for muted earthy tones. The survey feedback painted a different picture. People love vibrant colors, playful patterns, and eye-catching designs. Apparently, only when it's someone else's. Luckily, our perpetually grumpy boss prevailed with his judgment.

 

***

And because this whole shebang has such momentum, we've struck deals with platforms like Roblox, Minecraft, or Fortnite in the gaming segment.

They've added Jacks with special features and, at times,

digitally limited quantities to their game characters and worlds.

Yes, you can use the umbrella as a weapon or float across a lava flow.

We share the proceeds, keeping tabs on the spread, likes, and trading activities. Even the traditional gaming industry is interested. No more building block world without Jacks, no dolls and game figures without Jacks.

New demographics are tapped, only to turn them into fans later.

***

Alfred, my neighbor, couldn't make up his mind about a Jack (umbrella). Since the hype, he always looks away awkwardly when we cross paths in the stairwell. But the other day, he couldn't dodge me. Unsolicited, he pointed out that a hand-held umbrella while shopping is rather impractical. He needs both hands to carry groceries home, which is why he opted for our model, the Jacket (rain cape).

Works for us.

***

Recently, I attended an open-air concert. It unexpectedly started raining, but the organizer was prepared. Instead of beer and pretzels, there were Jackets branded with the band's name. It was fantastic for all involved. Umbrellas would've blocked the view, but Jackets kept the crowd dry and served as a lovely keepsake.

By the way, Jackets have now proven themselves in the wind too, especially after our characters showcased it in Season Five.

***

The initial concerns about dwindling rainfall rendering umbrellas obsolete have dissipated. Umbrellas are now part of the everyday street scene. Worldwide.

An expression of status and culture.

***

They're for aristocrats and punks, important people, those who think they are, wallflowers, life artists, comfort zone dwellers, and underground divers. With integrated smartphone or smartwatch features and a large holographic projection in open display mode. No wish goes unfulfilled.

Under a slightly transparent Jack, we can listen to our favorite podcast via a sound shower while the sun beats down relentlessly.  Jack is featured to charge and provide power for every demand.

***

The latter gadgets took the market by storm when we, in true old-school fashion, handed them to customers in stores and large department stores. Yes, with their own account login and the chance to try it out themselves. Many took them home right away. Yes, yes, we're talking at least eight hundred dollars each. The promotion tour on trains and buses was highly successful.

Yes, the umbrella isn't just an umbrella anymore. The days when it rains can be counted on two hands. In any case, it's a good idea to have your Jack with you. One thing has changed, though. Jack in my video game gains new joy when it rains.

But I must build him a Jack so he doesn't wither in the sun. Cheers!
